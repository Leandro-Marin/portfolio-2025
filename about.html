LEANDRO MARIN

Nonconformist and curious since the summer of 1992. I've never been a fan of serious or boring things—predictability just isn't my thing. My mind is a chaos of ideas, typography, colors, and references that blend together like brushstrokes in a Basquiat painting. Designing is the only way I have to shape my world, to turn all that disorder into something meaningful.

My creativity is a remix of everything around me. I find inspiration in Studio Ghibli's dreamlike worlds, the neon glow of a city that never sleeps, and the immersive sounds of lo-fi music. I'm not just a visual designer; I'm also a UX/UI designer, deeply passionate about digital spaces—the intersection where design and technology come together to bring new universes to life. I love the idea of creating experiences that aren't just seen but felt, that pull you in and make you forget you're looking at a screen.

Beyond design, I'm passionate about learning languages and exploring the world. I've always dreamed of doing like Willy Fog and traveling around the world in 80 days, visiting every corner of the planet. For me, traveling isn't just about moving from one place to another—it's about absorbing cultures, discovering stories, and noticing the details that others overlook. Every language I learn, every city I get lost in, every place I discover becomes another piece of how I create.

If I had a time machine, I'd head straight to the late '80s and early '90s, to the graffiti-covered streets of Keith Haring and the studio of Warhol, where art and provocation went hand in hand. I'd lose myself in bold colors, raw strokes, and an untamed creative energy. But for now, I'm here—designing, exploring, and always looking for ways to push my ideas one step further.
